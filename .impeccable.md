## Design Context

### Users
- **Front desk staff**: Use desktop workstations and wall-mounted/kiosk tablets daily. Primary job: check visitors in and out, manage walk-ins, monitor who is on site. High-frequency, task-focused sessions with hands often busy — touch targets must be generous.
- **Employees**: Use mobile phones primarily. Review visit approvals, track their own scheduled visits. Short, infrequent sessions.
- **Branch admin**: Use mobile phones primarily. Monitor dashboards and reports. Supervisory access, occasional visits to the app.

### Brand Personality
GMMCO Limited (CKA Birla Group) — large Indian industrial conglomerate. The interface manages physical security and facility access.
Three words: **Reliable. Clear. Efficient.**
Emotional goal: Confidence and control — users must feel they have complete, accurate visibility over who is in the facility at any moment. Errors or confusion in this UI have real-world safety consequences.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Established design system — do not deviate from it.** Brand red #EB2128, warm zinc neutrals, Inter font (mandated by client), dark chrome sidebar, light content surface.
- **Light mode only** — the app runs in well-lit reception, office, and factory environments. Dark mode is not a use case.
- **Utilitarian with polish** — information-dense but not cluttered. Every element must earn its place. Decorative elements have no role here.
- Anti-references: consumer app gradients, glassmorphism, heavy animations, playful iconography, dark-mode-first aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Information hierarchy over decoration** — the most urgent action or data point (overdue visitor, pending approval) must be instantly visible without scanning the page.
2. **Touch-first on tablet** — front desk kiosk tablets are primary devices. 44px minimum touch targets, comfortable row spacing, no hover-only interactions.
3. **Progressive disclosure** — show summary state first (KPI count, name, status), expand for detail on demand. Avoids overwhelming mobile/tablet users.
4. **Context-appropriate density** — desktop can be dense with a 60/40 split panel layout; tablet stacks panels vertically; mobile uses card lists instead of tables.
5. **Adapt, don't amputate** — all functionality must be reachable on every device. Mobile hides nothing critical; it just presents it differently.
